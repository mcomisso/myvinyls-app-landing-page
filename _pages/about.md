# About My Vinyls

My Vinyls is an iOS app built for people who love collecting records. It helps you catalog what you own, scan new additions in seconds, and keep everything synced with Discogs so your collection stays accurate everywhere.

The app was created by Matteo Comisso, a vinyl collector who wanted a fast, beautiful way to manage a growing library without the friction of spreadsheets or clunky tools. My Vinyls focuses on speed, clarity, and delight so you can spend less time organizing and more time listening.

There are plenty of ways to keep track of a record collection, but most of them feel like they were designed for data entry rather than for people who actually enjoy holding a record in their hands. My Vinyls takes a different approach. The app is built around the experience of collecting: flipping through your shelves, admiring artwork, remembering where you found a particular pressing. It treats your collection as something worth looking at, not just a list of catalog numbers.

Discogs integration is at the core of the app. Your collection syncs seamlessly, so anything you add or update in My Vinyls stays in step with your Discogs profile. You can search the entire Discogs database, scan barcodes to add records instantly, and browse detailed release information without leaving the app. If Discogs is where your collection lives online, My Vinyls is how you interact with it on your phone.

As a collector myself, I know the frustration of slow, cluttered apps that get in the way of something that should be enjoyable. My Vinyls is designed to be fast. Scrolling through hundreds of records feels smooth, searches return results immediately, and the interface stays clean no matter how large your library gets. Every design decision comes from actually using the app day to day, adding new finds from record shops, checking pressings at a flea market, or just browsing the collection at home.

The app is developed and maintained as a solo project, which means every feature gets careful attention and every piece of feedback matters. I use My Vinyls to manage my own collection, so improvements come from real needs rather than guesswork.
