Getting Started

My Vinyl+ is a companion app for Discogs, the world's largest music database. It allows you to manage your vinyl record collection, track your wantlist, search for records, scan barcodes, and discover new music - all from your iPhone or iPad.

When you first open the app, you'll be prompted to log in with your Discogs account. Tap "Log In with Discogs" and you'll be redirected to the Discogs website to authorize My Vinyl+. Once authorized, you'll be returned to the app with your collection ready to view.

Yes! My Vinyl+ uses the official Discogs OAuth system, which means we never see or store your Discogs password. All changes you make in the app are synced directly with Discogs servers. Your collection data is also cached locally on your device for faster access.

The app has 5 main tabs:

  • Discover - Daily recommendations and featured records
  • Collection - Your vinyl library
  • Wantlist - Records you want to buy
  • Marketplace - Browse and buy records
  • Search - Find any record on Discogs

Collection

There are several ways to add records:

  1. Search: Go to the Search tab, find the record, tap it, then tap "Add to Collection"
  2. Barcode Scan: Tap the camera icon in Search, scan the barcode on your record, and add it from the results
  3. Bulk Scanner: Use the bulk scanner (in Search) to scan multiple barcodes at once, then add them all together

Open the record details by tapping on it in your Collection. Scroll down and tap the "Remove from Collection" button. You'll be asked to confirm before the record is removed. This will also remove it from your Discogs collection.

Go to your Collection tab and tap the chart/stats icon in the navigation bar. This opens the Dashboard where you can see your collection value, total records, format breakdown, rarity index, and recent additions.

Yes! In the Collection tab, use the sort button to order by: Date Added, Artist Name (A-Z or Z-A), Title, Year, and more. You can also filter by format, genre, or folder.

Your collection value is shown in the Dashboard (tap the chart icon in Collection). The value is calculated using Discogs' median prices. For real-time price tracking, enable price notifications in Settings > Integrations > Price Tracking.

When you first load the app or after adding many records, the app syncs your collection with Discogs. This ensures your local data matches your online collection. The syncing indicator shows the progress. Your collection is fully usable during sync.

In your Collection, tap the view mode button in the toolbar to toggle between grid view (album covers) and list view (detailed rows with artist and title).

Wantlist

The Wantlist is your wishlist of records you want to buy. It syncs with your Discogs wantlist, so any changes you make appear both in the app and on the Discogs website.

Search for a record and tap on it to view details. Tap "Add to Wantlist" to add it. You can also add from the Discover tab or when viewing marketplace listings.

Open the record from your Wantlist tab, then tap "Remove from Wantlist". Alternatively, swipe left on the record in the list view and tap Delete.

Yes! Tap on any wantlist item to see current marketplace listings. You'll see how many copies are for sale and the price range. This helps you find the best deal for records you want.

When you've purchased a record, open it from your Wantlist and tap "Add to Collection". The record will be added to your collection. You can then remove it from your Wantlist if you wish (it won't be removed automatically).

Search & Scanning

Go to the Search tab and type an artist name, album title, or catalog number. Results appear as you type. Tap a result to see full details, tracklist, and add to your collection or wantlist.

In the Search tab, tap the camera icon to open the barcode scanner. Point your camera at the barcode on your record. When detected, the app will search for matching releases. This works with both standard barcodes and matrix numbers.

The Bulk Scanner lets you scan multiple records at once. Access it from the Search tab. Scan several barcodes in a row - they'll be queued up. When done scanning, review all the records and add them to your collection in one batch. This is perfect for cataloging large collections.

Yes! Tap the Shazam icon in the Search tab. Play the music you want to identify, and the app will recognize the song. Once identified, you can search for vinyl releases of that track.

Platinum subscribers can use advanced filters to narrow searches by format (LP, 7", CD, etc.), year range, genre, country, and catalog number. These help you find exactly the pressing you're looking for.

Discogs has millions of releases, and sometimes the exact pressing you have might not be listed. Try searching by catalog number (found on the label or spine) or matrix number (etched into the vinyl near the label). You can also browse all versions of a release by tapping "View Other Versions".

Folders

Folders help you organize your collection. Create folders like "Jazz", "Recently Added", "To Listen", or "Favorites". Records can be in multiple folders. Folders sync with your Discogs account.

Go to Settings > Collection > Folders (or access from the Collection tab menu). Tap the "+" button and enter a folder name. The folder will be created both in the app and on Discogs.

Open a record from your Collection, scroll to the Folders section, and select which folders to add it to. You can also long-press a record in the grid/list view for quick folder access.

In the Collection tab, tap the folder icon or filter button and select the folder you want to view. Only records in that folder will be shown. Tap "All" to return to viewing your complete collection.

Yes. Go to Settings > Collection > Folders, swipe left on the folder you want to delete, and tap Delete. Records in that folder won't be deleted from your collection - they'll just be unassigned from that folder.

Sync & Data

My Vinyl+ keeps your collection in sync with Discogs. When you add, remove, or modify records, changes are sent to Discogs immediately. The app also periodically checks for changes made on the Discogs website or other apps.

Smart Sync is a background feature that automatically updates your collection data at a scheduled time each day. Enable it in Settings > Collection > Smart Sync. This ensures your collection value and details are always up to date.

Go to Settings > Collection > Memory Management. Here you can clear:

  • Collection cache (re-downloads your collection)
  • Wantlist cache (re-downloads your wantlist)
  • Release details cache (clears stored album info)
  • All caches at once

This is helpful if data seems incorrect or outdated.

No! Clearing the cache only removes locally stored data. Your actual collection lives on Discogs servers and will be re-downloaded when you open the app. This is completely safe and won't affect your Discogs account.

Go to Settings > Collection > Export Data (Platinum feature). Choose what to export (collection, wantlist, or both) and which fields to include. The export is saved as a CSV file that you can open in Excel, Numbers, or any spreadsheet app.

Try these steps:

  1. Pull down on the Collection screen to force refresh
  2. Go to Settings > Collection > Memory Management and clear the collection cache
  3. Check your internet connection
  4. Log out and log back in (Settings > Accounts)

Music Playback

Yes! My Vinyl+ integrates with streaming services. When viewing a record, tap the Play button to listen to it on your connected music service. This is great for previewing records before buying or for digital listening when you're away from your turntable.

My Vinyl+ supports:

  • Apple Music
  • Spotify
  • Tidal
  • YouTube Music

Connect your preferred service in Settings > Integrations > Music Playback.

Go to Settings > Integrations > Music Playback and select Spotify. You'll be redirected to log in with your Spotify account. Once connected, you can play tracks directly from record detail pages.

Scrobbling logs the music you listen to on Last.fm, building a history of your listening habits. When enabled, My Vinyl+ can scrobble vinyl listening sessions. Connect your Last.fm account in Settings > Integrations.

With Last.fm connected, open a record from your collection and use the scrobble feature. You can log individual tracks or entire albums. This creates a log on Last.fm showing that you listened to that record.

Marketplace

The Marketplace tab lets you browse records for sale on Discogs. You can find listings for records in your wantlist, discover sellers worldwide, and view pricing information.

You can browse listings and view details, but purchases are completed on the Discogs website. Tap a listing to view it on Discogs where you can add to cart and checkout securely.

Open any record (from search, wantlist, or collection) and scroll to the Marketplace section. You'll see how many copies are for sale and their price range. Tap to view all listings with seller ratings and shipping options.

Price alerts notify you when a record in your wantlist drops to your target price. Set them up in Settings > Integrations > Price Tracking. This is a Platinum feature that helps you snag deals on records you want.

Collection value uses Discogs' median sale prices for each record in your collection. This is based on actual sales data from the Discogs marketplace. Values update regularly to reflect current market prices.

Platinum Subscription

Platinum is the premium tier of My Vinyl+. It unlocks advanced features like bulk scanning, advanced search filters, custom themes, unlimited price tracking, collection export, and more. It helps support the ongoing development of the app.

Platinum features include:

  • Advanced search filters
  • Bulk barcode scanner
  • Custom accent colors
  • Tab bar customization
  • Price drop alerts (unlimited)
  • Collection export to CSV
  • Custom record fields
  • Multiple Discogs accounts

Yes! New users get a 2-week free trial of Platinum features. This lets you experience everything My Vinyl+ has to offer before deciding to subscribe.

Go to Settings and tap "Subscribe to Platinum" or look for the Platinum badge next to locked features. Choose monthly or yearly billing. Payment is handled securely through Apple's App Store.

Subscriptions are managed through your Apple ID. Go to iPhone Settings > [Your Name] > Subscriptions > My Vinyl+ and tap Cancel. You'll keep Platinum access until the end of your billing period.

Yes! If you've previously subscribed, go to Settings and tap "Restore Purchases". Make sure you're signed in with the same Apple ID you used to subscribe. Your Platinum access will be restored.

Go to Settings and look for "Redeem Code" in the subscription section. Enter your promotional code to activate Platinum features.

Multiple Accounts

Yes! Platinum subscribers can link multiple Discogs accounts. This is useful if you have separate accounts for different collections, or if family members share a device.

Go to Settings > Accounts > Add Account. You'll go through the Discogs login process for your additional account. Once added, you can switch between accounts with a tap.

Go to Settings > Accounts and tap the account you want to switch to. Alternatively, on iPad, use the account switcher in the sidebar. Your collection and wantlist will update to show the selected account's data.

Yes! Each account's collection, wantlist, folders, and settings are kept completely separate. Switching accounts loads that account's data. Nothing is mixed or shared between accounts.

Go to Settings > Accounts, swipe left on the account you want to remove, and tap Log Out. This removes the account from the app but doesn't delete your Discogs account or data.

Appearance

Go to Settings > Appearance > Theme Color (Platinum feature). Choose from a variety of accent colors that will be applied throughout the app. The color affects buttons, highlights, and icons.

Go to Settings > Appearance > App Icon. Choose from many icon designs including classic, modern, seasonal, and special editions. Your choice appears on your home screen.

Yes! Go to Settings > Appearance > Tab Bar (Platinum feature). Toggle off any tabs you don't want to see. You can always re-enable them later.

Go to Settings > Appearance > Text Size. Use the slider to increase or decrease text size throughout the app. This is independent of your system text size setting.

Yes! My Vinyl+ automatically adapts to your system appearance setting. If your iPhone/iPad is in Dark Mode, the app will display in Dark Mode. There's no separate in-app toggle - it follows your system preference.

Widgets

My Vinyl+ offers several home screen widgets:

  • Random Record: Shows a random album from your collection
  • Today's Album: Daily recommendation from '1001 Albums' list
  • Collection Value: Current value of your collection
  • Collection Counter: Number of records owned
  • Last Added: Most recently added record
  • Selectable Record: Choose a specific album to display

Long-press on your home screen, tap the "+" button, search for "My Vinyl+", select a widget size, and tap "Add Widget". You can then position it on your home screen.

Go to Settings > Advanced > Widget Configuration. Here you can select which collection folder to use for the Random Record widget, and configure other widget-specific options.

iOS controls when widgets refresh to save battery. Widgets update periodically (not in real-time). To force an update, open the app briefly. If a widget shows old data, try removing and re-adding it.

Troubleshooting

Try these steps:

  1. Force quit the app and reopen it
  2. Clear the cache in Settings > Collection > Memory Management
  3. Make sure you're on a stable internet connection
  4. Restart your device
  5. Update to the latest version of the app

Check your internet connection first. If connected, try:

  1. Pull down to refresh the collection
  2. Go to Settings > Collection > Memory Management and clear the collection cache
  3. Log out and log back in
  4. Check if Discogs.com is accessible (it may be down)

Ensure you've granted camera permission (Settings app > My Vinyl+ > Camera). Make sure there's adequate lighting. Hold your phone steady about 6-12 inches from the barcode. If a barcode still won't scan, try manually entering the number in search.

Check notification permissions in the Settings app > My Vinyl+ > Notifications. Also verify your notification preferences in the app under Settings > Notifications. Make sure "Do Not Disturb" isn't blocking alerts.

Go to Settings > Help & Support > Send Feedback. Describe the issue or feature request in detail. Include steps to reproduce bugs. Screenshots are helpful! We read every message and appreciate your feedback.

Go to Settings > Accounts, swipe left on your account, and tap Log Out. This will remove your data from the app but won't affect your Discogs account. You can log back in anytime.

Records removed from your collection are also removed from Discogs. You'll need to search for the record and add it again. Unfortunately, there's no undo function as changes sync immediately with Discogs.

If you have analytics enabled (Settings > Help & Support > Privacy), crash reports are sent automatically. When contacting support, include:

  • What you were doing when it crashed
  • Your device model and iOS version
  • App version (Settings > Help & Support > About)
  • Whether it happens consistently
