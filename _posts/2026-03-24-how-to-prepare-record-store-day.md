There's a particular kind of defeat that only vinyl collectors understand: getting home from Record Store Day, buzzing with excitement, sliding a new record onto the shelf, and realising you already own it. Different pressing, same album. Forty quid, gone.

It happens more than anyone likes to admit. And it's entirely preventable.

RSD 2026 is on April 18. Here's how to walk in prepared and walk out with only the records you actually wanted.

## Two Weeks Before: Know What You Own

This is the step everyone skips, and it's the most important one.

Sit down and go through your collection. Not a casual glance at the shelf. An actual inventory. You're looking for two things: what you already own (so you don't rebuy it), and gaps you'd like to fill (so you have targets beyond the RSD exclusives).

If your collection is under 50 records, you can probably keep this in your head. Once you're past a hundred, memory gets unreliable. Past three hundred, you're guessing.

The fastest approach is using a collection management app. [My Vinyl+](https://apps.apple.com/app/apple-store/id1547173908?pt=1339105&ct=website&mt=8) lets you scan barcodes to log records in seconds and pulls in full release details from Discogs, so you always have a searchable inventory in your pocket. When you're standing in a shop on April 18 wondering "do I own this pressing?", you can check in five seconds instead of gambling.

However you do it, digitally or with a spreadsheet or even a handwritten list, the point is the same: go in knowing what you have.

## One Week Before: Build Your Want List

The full RSD 2026 catalogue has been out since early March. Go through it. All of it. Yes, it's long. That's the homework.

Rank your targets into three tiers:

**Must-have:** Two or three titles you'll be gutted to miss. These define your strategy. You're queuing early for these.

**Would-be-nice:** Five to eight titles you'd happily pick up if they're available. No heartbreak if you miss them.

**Impulse zone:** Everything else that caught your eye. These are the "if I see it, sure" titles. Don't queue for them.

Write this list down. On your phone, on paper, whatever works. Having it in your hand when you walk in means you spend less time deliberating and more time acting.

## Three Days Before: Research Your Stores

Not every shop gets every title. Call ahead or check social media. Most participating stores post updates about which RSD titles they've ordered and what quantities to expect.

Key things to find out:

- What time are they opening?
- Are they doing a queue system or first-come-first-served?
- Do they have purchase limits per title?
- Are they stocking the specific releases on your must-have list?

If you're in a city with multiple indie shops, plan a route. Store A might get the title Store B didn't order, and vice versa. Having a second stop gives you a backup plan.

## The Night Before: Pack Your Kit

This sounds excessive until you've done an RSD without it:

**Cash.** Enough for your must-haves plus a buffer. Card machines fail under pressure.

**A tote bag or record bag.** You'll be carrying purchases around, possibly to a second shop. Plastic carrier bags and vinyl don't mix well in a crowd.

**Your want list.** On your phone, on a card in your pocket, wherever.

**Your collection inventory.** Again, phone app or written list. This is your duplicate shield.

**A bottle of water and a snack.** You might be queuing for an hour or more. A banana and some water turn a miserable wait into a manageable one.

**Comfortable shoes.** You'll be standing, then walking, then standing some more. Leave the fashion trainers at home.

## On the Day: The Actual Strategy

Get there early. If the shop opens at 8, aim to be in line by 7 at the latest for the high-demand titles. Earlier if you're after the Taylor Swift 7-inch or similar headliners.

When you get inside, go straight to your must-haves. Browse later. The first five minutes are about securing the titles that will sell out. Everything else can wait.

Check condition before you buy. RSD releases sometimes have sleeve damage from shipping and handling. A quick look at the corners and a peek at the disc takes ten seconds and saves regret.

If your first-choice shop is out of something, move to your backup. Don't linger mourning what's gone. The clock is ticking at the next store too.

## After the Rush: The Afternoon Browse

Once the frenzy dies down, usually by late morning, go back and browse properly. Many shops have sales on regular stock during RSD, and the atmosphere shifts from competitive to communal. This is when the day gets genuinely enjoyable.

Talk to other collectors. Ask the staff what they're excited about. Dig through the non-RSD new arrivals. Some of the best records I've ever bought on Record Store Day weren't RSD exclusives at all.

## The Duplicate Problem, Solved

It comes down to one thing: knowing your collection before you walk through the door.

The embarrassment of buying a duplicate isn't about the money (though that stings too). It's about wasting one of your limited opportunities on something you already had, while the record you actually needed went to someone else.

Inventory your collection. Make your list. Check it twice. Then go have a brilliant day.

Record Store Day 2026 is April 18. You've got three weeks. That's more than enough time to be ready.
