Spring is shaping up to be one of the busiest stretches for new vinyl in recent memory. Between major label releases, indie comebacks, and some genuinely surprising debuts, there's a lot competing for your turntable time (and shelf space) over the next few months.

Here's what's worth watching — and pre-ordering before stock disappears.

## March: Already Dropping

The tail end of March alone has been stacked. **Courtney Barnett** returned with *Creature of Habit* on March 27th, her first solo album since *Things Take Time, Take Time*. If her earlier records are anything to go by, the vinyl variants will move fast.

**RAYE** released *This Music May Contain Hope* the same week. After her 2023 breakthrough with *My 21st Century Blues* swept the BRITs, demand for her vinyl has been consistently high. Expect limited colored pressings to be gone within days.

**Tom Misch** dropped *Full Circle* on March 27th too — a title that feels appropriate for the jazz-funk guitarist whose debut *Geography* became a quiet vinyl collector favorite. And **Robyn** came back with *Sexistential*, her first album since 2018's *Honey*. Eight years between albums tends to build up some anticipation.

## April: The Heavy Hitters

This is where it gets intense.

**Arlo Parks** kicks off April with *Ambiguous Desire* on the 3rd, apparently pivoting from indie-pop into club territory — think garage, two-step, Jamie xx influences. A big sonic shift that could make this a standout pressing.

**Jessie Ware** follows on April 10th with *Superbloom*. After *That! Feels Good!* became one of 2023's most played vinyl records in collector circles, this one's almost guaranteed to get the deluxe treatment.

Mid-April brings **Honey Dijon's** *Nightlife* (April 17th) and **ZAYN's** *KONNAKOL* the same day. Love or hate the former One Direction member, his vinyl releases sell.

Then April 24th hits with a triple threat: **Foo Fighters** with *Your Favorite Toy*, **Noah Kahan** with *The Great Divide*, and **Kneecap** with *FENIAN*. The Foo Fighters alone will probably account for a significant chunk of April's vinyl sales. Noah Kahan's folk-pop has developed a surprisingly dedicated vinyl following. And Kneecap — the Irish-language hip-hop group turned film stars — have the kind of cult momentum that turns first pressings into collector items.

## May: The Deep Cuts

May 1st is stacked for collectors who lean indie. **American Football** releases *LP4* — their fourth album, and given the Midwest emo revival that's been building for years, demand will be high. Their self-titled debut is already one of the most sought-after emo pressings. **The Black Keys** drop *Peaches!* the same day, along with **Tori Amos** with *In Times of Dragons*.

A week later, **Lykke Li** drops *The Afterparty* on May 8th, which she's calling her final album. Last albums from beloved artists have a way of becoming collector pieces. **Muna** follows with *Dancing on the Wall* the same week.

Later in May, **Bleachers** releases *Everyone For 10 Minutes* on the 22nd, rounding out what might be the strongest spring release calendar in years.

## What About Reissues?

Beyond new albums, keep an eye on the **Queen II Collector's Edition** — a massive box set featuring a 2026 remix from the original master tapes, session tracks, alternate takes, demos, BBC sessions, and live recordings from 1973 and 1974. It includes two LPs plus five CDs and a 112-page book. For Queen collectors, this is the reissue event of the year.

**Smashing Pumpkins** are also getting a *Gish* anniversary reissue in late May with multiple vinyl color variants.

## How to Actually Get These

A few practical tips for anyone trying to secure copies:

**Pre-order early.** Indie pressings especially have limited runs. Retailers like Rough Trade, Banquet Records, and your local independent shop often get exclusive color variants that sell out on announcement day.

**Watch for indie-exclusive variants.** Many of these albums will have standard black vinyl available indefinitely, but the limited colored pressings — often exclusive to independent record stores — are what collectors want. These typically get one production run and that's it.

**Set up alerts.** Follow your favorite shops on social media. Most announce pre-orders there before updating their websites. A five-minute head start matters when runs are 500-1000 copies.

**Don't sleep on the smaller releases.** Everyone will be fighting over Foo Fighters and RAYE. The records that appreciate in value tend to be the ones people overlooked — the Arlo Parks sonic pivot, the American Football LP4, the Kneecap pressing that nobody expected to sell out.

Spring 2026 is going to put some real pressure on your record shelf. Plan accordingly.
